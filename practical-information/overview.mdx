# Practical Information for Daily Life

Making Bodrum home requires knowing how things work. This section covers daily life essentials.

## Essential Quick Reference

### Emergency Numbers

| Service | Number | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| **Emergency (Police/Ambulance/Fire)** | 112 | 24/7, English usually available |
| **Police Non-emergency** | 155 | Local police station |
| **Ambulance Only** | 112 | Or call from hospital directly |
| **Fire** | 110 | Or use 112 |
| **Traffic Police** | 154 | Traffic accidents/violations |
| **Tourist Police** | 155 (Turizm Polisi subunit) | English-speaking |

### Important Contacts

- **Your Embassy:** Keep number handy (in your phone)
- **Your Bank:** For card issues, account help
- **Utilities:** Electric/water company emergency lines
- **Doctor:** Save favorite clinic number
- **Landlord:** Emergency contact

## Shopping & Superstores

### Main Supermarkets

**Carrefour** - Turkish chain
- Full grocery selection
- European quality items
- Multiple locations in Bodrum
- Hours: Usually 9 AM - 9 PM

**Migros** - Turkish chain  
- Slightly higher-end
- Good bakery
- Multiple locations
- Hours: Usually 8 AM - 9 PM

**Sok** - Budget chain
- Good prices
- More basic selection
- Limited locations
- Good for staples

**Local grocers** - Small neighborhood shops
- Fresh produce
- Personal service
- Higher prices typically
- Support local economy

### Shopping Tips

**Best times:**
- Early morning (freshest produce)
- Weekday afternoons (less crowded)
- Avoid peak times (8-9 AM, 6-7 PM)

**Markets (Pazarlar):**
- Weekly farmers markets (Saturday is main day)
- Fresh produce, excellent prices
- Haggling acceptable for some items
- Experience the local culture

**Opening hours:**
- Most shops close 1-4 PM (siesta)
- Some close completely, others open mid-afternoon
- Plan accordingly

## Time Orientation

### Turkish Business Hours

**Banks:**
- 9 AM - 5 PM weekdays
- Closed Saturdays/Sundays
- Lunch break 12-1:30 PM sometimes

**Government Offices:**
- 9 AM - 5 PM weekdays
- Closest before 11 AM (less crowded)
- Actually faster than you'd think

**Shops:**
- Highly variable
- Many closed 1-4 PM
- Evening hours until 8-9 PM
- Sundays: Mixed (some closed, touristy areas open)

**Restaurants:**
- Breakfast/tea: 6-10 AM
- Lunch: 12-2:30 PM (peak)
- Dinner: 7-11 PM (peak at 8-9 PM)
- Many close between 4-7 PM

**Turkish Time Concept:**
- "Soon" might mean hours
- Appointments might be 15 min late (normal)
- Patience required
- Flexibility rewarded

### Time Zones

**Turkey:** UTC+3 (year-round, no daylight saving as of recent years)

**Compared to:**
- UAE: 1 hour behind
- Qatar: 1 hour behind
- UK: 2 hours ahead
- US East Coast: 7 hours ahead
- US West Coast: 10 hours ahead

## Post & Mail

### Turkish Postal Service (PTT)

**Basic service:**
- Affordable ($1-5 for letters internationally)
- Slow (2-4 weeks typical for international)
- Reliable enough for non-critical items

**Where:**
- Post offices throughout Bodrum
- Village post offices in neighborhoods
- PTT window at hotels

**For receiving mail:**
- Can use "Poste Restante" (hold at location)
- Or have address (hotel, apartment)
- Some places resist receiving mail if not official resident

### Packages

**International:**
- DHL, FedEx, UPS available
- More expensive but faster (3-7 days)
- Reliable for important items
- Requires tracking

**From abroad:**
- Use English name + address clearly
- Turkish transliteration helps
- May take time in customs

### Practical approach:
- Email/digital communication primary
- Mail for non-critical items only
- Use courier for important documents
- Save shipping costs by postal service for bulk orders

## Government Offices Hours

**Municipality (Belediye):**
- 9 AM - 5 PM M-F
- Some accept appointment requests
- Arrive early for shorter wait

**Police/Security (Emniyet/Muhtar):**
- 9 AM - 5 PM (though some 24/7 at main station)
- Neighborhood chief (Muhtar): Variable hours
- Appointment system sometimes available

**Tax Office (Vergi Müdürlüğü):**
- 9 AM - 5 PM M-F
- Come early (fill up by 10 AM)
- Straightforward processes usually quick

**Immigration (Göç İdaresi):**
- 9 AM - 5 PM M-F
- Appointment recommended
- Phone to confirm hours/services

**Pro tips:**
- Always bring multiple copies of documents
- Arrive early (best time 9-10:30 AM)
- Have appointment if possible
- Bring translator if unsure of Turkish

## Practical Skills to Develop

### Basic Turkish Phrases

See [Language Guide](/practical-information/language) for more, but essential phrases:

- **Merhaba** (Hello)
- **Teşekkür ederim** (Thank you)
- **Lütfen** (Please)
- **Evet/Hayır** (Yes/No)
- **Biraz İngilizce konuşur musunuz?** (Do you speak English?)
- **Hesap lütfen** (Check please - at restaurant)
- **Afedersiniz** (Excuse me)

### Practical Local Knowledge

**Buying from locals:**
- Greeting/politeness matters (greet, don't just demand)
- Haggling acceptable at markets (not shops)
- Building relationships leads to better deals

**Negotiating:**
- Turkish culture values negotiation
- Always ask if price negotiable (especially rentals, services)
- First offer often not final
- Relationships matter more than price

**Respect:**
- Remove shoes when entering homes
- Don't point with one finger (use full hand or beck)
- Show respect for elders
- Religious sites: Modest dress, remove shoes
- Ask before photographing people

## Practical Problem-Solving

When issues arise (they will):

**First step:** Ask other expats
- Facebook groups
- Meetup groups
- Coffee shop regulars
- They've likely faced same issue

**Second step:** Ask locals
- Neighborhood shopkeeper
- Landlord/property manager
- Turkish friends
- They know local resources

**Third step:** Seek professional help
- Lawyer ($30-50/hour initial consultation)
- Accountant for financial issues
- Doctor for health issues
- Police for safety issues

**Reality:** Most issues resolve quickly with patience and right connections
