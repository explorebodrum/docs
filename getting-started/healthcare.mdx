# Healthcare System in Bodrum

Healthcare is excellent in Bodrum—modern, affordable, and accessible. Understanding the system is important for your relocation.

## Healthcare Options at a Glance

| Type | Cost | Quality | Best For |
|------|------|---------|----------|
| **Private Clinics** | $30-80/visit | Excellent | Routine care, comfort |
| **Private Hospitals** | $100-500/visit | Excellent | Serious issues, surgeries |
| **Government Hospitals** | Free-minimal | Good | Large medical issues |
| **Telemedicine** | $20-40 | Good | Consultations, minor |

**Most expats use:** Private clinics (best balance of cost/quality)

## Health Insurance (Required for Residence Permit)

### Types of Insurance

**Turkish Insurance (Cheapest)**
- Cost: $15-30/month
- Covers: Basic medical, some hospitalization
- For: Meeting legal requirements
- Where: Turkish insurance companies (Eurocross, Acibadem, Allianz Turkey)

**International Insurance**
- Cost: $50-150+/month
- Covers: Comprehensive, worldwide coverage, evacuation
- For: Peace of mind, comprehensive coverage
- Where: International insurance providers (Allianz, BUPA, etc.)

**Hybrid Approach (Recommended)**
- Turkish base insurance ($20-30/month)
- International travel/evacuation add-on ($30-50/month)
- Total: $50-80/month, comprehensive coverage

**Government Insurance (SGK)**
- For: Turkish employees or self-employed paying taxes
- Cost: ~$60-100/month (mandatory)
- Covers: State system benefits
- Automatic if: Employee or registered self-employed

### Getting Insurance for Residence Permit

You MUST have insurance to get residence permit. Process:

1. **Get Turkish Insurance Letter**
   - Email Turkish company: "I need health insurance for residence permit"
   - They send quote (~$20-30/month minimum)
   - You apply online (takes 1 day)
   - You get confirmation letter (immediately by email)
   - You have coverage letter for immigration office

2. **Timeline:** Can be done in 3-5 days

3. **Cost:** ~$20-30/month minimum

4. **Recommended Providers (Turkish-based):**
   - Eurocross: Good coverage, expat-friendly
   - Acibadem: Excellent network
   - Allianz Turkey: International partnership

<Callout type="tip">
**Pro Tip:** Start with cheap Turkish insurance ($20-30) just for residence permit. Upgrade to international later if desired. Insurance is available anytime, no rush.
</Callout>

## Private Clinics in Bodrum

### Main Hospitals/Medical Centers

**Bodrum Private Hospital (Bodrum Özel Hastanesi)**
- Location: Central Bodrum
- Quality: Excellent (JCI accredited)
- Services: Full range, 24/7 emergency
- Equipment: Modern, CT/ultrasound/lab on-site
- Cost: Typical for private (emergency ~$100-200 initial)

**Acibadem/Eurocross Bodrum**
- Network clinic
- Quality: Excellent
- Services: Comprehensive outpatient
- Cost: Standard private rates

**Other Private Clinics**
- Multiple independent clinics throughout Bodrum
- Quality: Generally good to excellent
- Cost: $30-80 for consultations

### Finding a Doctor

**English-Speaking Doctors:**
- Many private clinic doctors speak English
- Ask specifically when calling: "Doktor İngilizce konuşuyor mu?"
- English speakers common in clinics catering to tourists

**Where to Find:**
- Ask property manager/landlord
- Ask other expats (Facebook groups)
- Call clinic ask if English available
- Most hotels have doctor recommendations

### Typical Clinic Visit

**Process:**
1. Call clinic, make appointment (same day usually possible)
2. Arrive 10-15 minutes early
3. Registration/payment info
4. Wait (usually 15-30 minutes)
5. See doctor (20-30 minutes)
6. Get prescription if needed
7. Pay bill ($30-80 typical)
8. Use prescription at pharmacy

**Cost Breakdown**
- Doctor visit: $30-60
- Tests (if needed): $20-100
- Medication: $5-30 typically

**Payment:** Cash or card accepted

## Government Healthcare (SGK)

### What is SGK?

Turkish government health insurance system (Sosyal Güvenlik Kurumu):
- Mandatory if you work/do business
- Optional enrollment possible
- Covers basic medical + hospitalization
- Network of clinics and hospitals

### Registering for SGK

**If Self-Employed:**
1. Register business (KVK)
2. Register with SGK office
3. Pay monthly contributions (~$60-100)
4. Get SGK social security number
5. Receive health coverage

**Process:** ~2-3 weeks, done by accountant usually

**Coverage:** Includes visits, surgeries, hospitalization (up to limits)

**Reality:** Many expats also keep private insurance (SGK is backup)

## Pharmacy (Eczane)

Turkish pharmacies are excellent:

- **Locations:** Everywhere (usually near clinics)
- **Hours:** Most open 9 AM - 8 PM, some 24/7
- **Medications:** Most available over-the-counter
- **Cost:** Cheap (generic drugs especially)
- **English:** Limited but usually understood

**How to Use:**
1. Get prescription from doctor (or just describe symptoms)
2. Go to pharmacy
3. Show prescription or describe issue
4. Pharmacist recommends medication
5. Pay ($5-30 typically)
6. Done

**Many medications available without prescription** that require prescription in US/UAE

**Bring from home:** Only critical prescription medications where Turkish equivalents problematic

**Where:**

## Specific Health Concerns

<Accordion title="What About COVID?">

**Current Situation (2024-2025):**
- Turkey handled COVID well
- Low ongoing cases
- Healthcare system excellent during pandemic
- Vaccines available
- No travel restrictions currently
- Masks rarely worn

**Recommendation:** Get COVID vaccination (if not already) before arriving. Available at any clinic in Bodrum if needed. Not currently a major concern.

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Tropical/Unusual Diseases?">

**Good News:** Bodrum is Mediterranean, not tropical.

**Diseases NOT a concern:**
- Malaria
- Dengue
- Yellow fever

**Diseases to BE AWARE OF:**
- Standard seasonal flus (winter)
- Occasional parasites (filtered water helps)

**Water Quality:** Tap water is safe to drink in Bodrum. Filtered water common.

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Women's Health">

**OB/GYN Services:** Excellent in Bodrum

**Available:**
- Birth control pills: OTC
- IUD insertion: Available private clinics
- Mammograms: Available
- Regular check-ups: Routine

**Cost:** 30-50% cheaper than UAE

**Finding:** Ask at private hospital for women's health specialists

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Prescriptions & Medications">

**Bringing from Home:**
- Prescription medications: Generally allowed
- Bring copies of prescriptions
- Generic names written down
- Usually 3-month supply max per customs rules

**Buying in Turkey:**
- Most medications available without prescription (even antibiotics sometimes)
- Pharmacists are knowledgeable, will recommend
- Turkish equivalents often same drug, different name
- Bring medication list to show pharmacist

**Prohibited Medications:**
- Some controlled drugs strictly regulated
- Narcotics subject to strict rules
- Anti-anxiety medications allowed with prescription

**Best Practice:** Get prescription from Turkish doctor, fills locally

</Accordion>

## Health Challenges During Relocation

### Jet Lag
- Arrive 1-2 days early
- Reset schedule (get sun light, sleep naturally)
- Melatonin helpful
- Usually passes within 3-5 days

### Stress & Adjustment
- Moving is stressful
- Allow time to settle
- Connect with community early
- Get into routine (help sleep)
- See doctor if persistent insomnia/anxiety

### Diet Adjustment
- Mediterranean diet generally healthier
- Fresh produce abundant
- May take week for stomach adjustment
- Probiotics/yogurt help

### Sun Exposure
- Mediterranean sun strong
- Sunburn common in expats new to region
- Use SPF 50+ daily
- Limit peak hours (11 AM - 4 PM)
- Protective clothing recommended

## Emergency Services

### Emergency Number
- **Dial 112** (ambulance, fire, police)
- English usually available
- Fast response in Bodrum
- Good hospitals with emergency departments

### Hospital Visits
- All major private hospitals: 24/7 emergency
- Wait: Usually 10-30 minutes for assessment
- Quality: Excellent
- Cost: Emergency visit $100-200, depending on treatment

### Evacuation Insurance
- If serious emergency, may need Istanbul hospital
- Medical evacuation available through international insurance
- Helicopter available if critical
- Plan for this with insurance
