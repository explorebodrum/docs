### Furnished vs. Unfurnished

**Furnished (Mobilyalı)**
- Includes: Basic furniture, kitchenware, sometimes appliances
- Cost: 15-30% premium
- Best for: Short-term renters, won't buy furniture
- Reality: Quality varies from basic to nice

**Unfurnished (Dönerme)**
- Bare walls, you buy everything
- Cost: Lower base rent
- Best for: Long-term, planning to invest in furniture
- Reality: You'll spend $500-2,000 on basic furniture setup

**Semi-furnished**
- Common middle ground
- Furniture but not full kitchenware
- Check specifically what's included

## Long-Term Rental: After Month 1

### Signed Rental Agreement

**Turkish Rental Contract (Kiralama Sözleşmesi)**

What's Important:

| Item | What to Look For |
|------|-----------------|
| **Rent Amount** | Monthly price clearly stated in Turkish Lira |
| **Utilities** | What's included? Water, electricity, gas, WiFi? |
| **Lease Term** | Usually 1 year (but can negotiate shorter) |
| **Deposit** | Typically 1-2 months (refundable) |
| **Increases** | Annual increase clause (negotiate limit - max 20% typical) |
| **Maintenance** | Who pays for repairs? (landlord usually) |
| **Cancellation** | Early termination terms (usually lose deposit) |
| **Late Payment** | Consequences (typically 2% penalty/day in Turkey) |

### Signature Types

**Protection Options:**

```
Option 1: Verbal Agreement + WhatsApp Confirmation
- Cheaper, flexible
- Risk: No official protection
- Fine if: Trust landlord, short-term

Option 2: Written Agreement (Both Sign)
- Standard, protective
- Risk: Minor (unlikely issues with Turkish rental law)
- Best for: Most situations

Option 3: Lawyer Review (~$40-80, optional)
- Extra protection
- Worth it if: Expensive property or long-term
- Recommended: First rental as expat
```

### Deposit & Payment

**Typical Process:**
1. Offer accepted
2. Landlord requests deposit + first month's rent
3. You transfer (often bank transfer, sometimes cash)
4. Get written receipt
5. Receive keys
6. Move in

**Safety Tips:**
- Use bank transfer (creates paper trail)
- Get written receipt with landlord signature
- Have photos of empty property (proof of condition)
- Get landlord contact info + emergency contact
- Meet landlord in person before transferring money

<Callout type="warning">
**Watch Out For:**

🚩 Landlord requesting payment before seeing property/meeting in person
🚩 No receipt offered for deposit
🚩 Pressure to pay in cash (insist on bank transfer)
🚩 Unusually low price (too good to be true often is)
🚩 Landlord unavailable/hard to contact
🚩 Property looks abandoned or unmaintained
</Callout>

### Red Flags & Scams

**Rental Scams Explained:**

Common scam:
- Fake landlord posts property on Airbnb
- You pay deposit + first month
- Landlord disappears
- Real owner shows up

**Prevention:**
- Meet landlord in person
- Verify ownership (ask to see ID + property deed photo)
- Use established platforms (Airbnb, Booking has some protection)
- If huge discount, be extra careful
- Ask on Facebook groups: "Is this landlord legitimate?"

## Moving In: What to Expect

### Rental Utilities Setup

After signing, you need to set up accounts:

**Electricity (Elektrik)**
- Account: TEDAŞ (state electric company)
- Process: 1-3 days
- Need: Rental contract, ID, utility reading
- Cost: Deposit (~same as 1-2 months planned usage) + monthly bill
- Billing: Billed monthly or bi-monthly

**Water (Su)**
- Account: Municipal water
- Process: 1-2 days
- Similar to electricity setup

**Gas (Doğalgaz)**
- Account: Natural gas if using
- Not all areas have gas (rural areas use propane tanks)
- Process: 2-3 days

**Internet (İnternet)**
- Provider: Turk Telecom (TTNet) is main, others available
- Speed: 50-100 Mbps typically available
- Cost: $20-40/month
- Setup: 1-2 days usually
- Critical for remote workers: TEST THIS before signing lease
- Some areas have fiber, others ADSL or wireless

**Phone (Telefon)**
- Get local Turkish SIM card immediately
- Providers: Vodafone, Turkcell, Türk Telecom
- Cost: ~$20/month for decent plan
- Activation: 15 minutes

### First-Month Tasks

- [ ] Set up electricity account
- [ ] Set up water account  
- [ ] Set up internet (TEST SPEED FIRST if remote working)
- [ ] Get Turkish phone SIM
- [ ] Introduce yourself to neighbors
- [ ] Get landlord/property manager contact
- [ ] Take photos of property condition (for deposit return)
- [ ] Understand how to pay rent (bank transfer usually)
- [ ] Get utility bill payment system set up
- [ ] Ask neighbors about any issues with utilities

## Negotiating Your Lease

### Tips for Better Deals

1. **Long-term lease:** 6-12 months = 10-20% discount
2. **Off-season rental:** Nov-Mar = 30-50% cheaper
3. **In-person meetings:** Landlords are friendly; explain situation
4. **Multiple months upfront:** Sometimes gives discount
5. **Referral from expat:** Landlords value problem-free tenants

### What's Negotiable

✓ Monthly rate (especially off-season)
✓ Lease term (shorter = higher, longer = lower)
✓ Furniture included/not included
✓ Utilities included
✗ Something obviously wrong with property

## Legalities & Taxes

### Staying Legal

If renting through official channel:
- Host must register rental income
- You're covered as tenant
- Deposit legally protected

If under-the-table:
- Common but technically not permitted
- Very low enforcement
- No protection for you
- Tax not paid

**Recommendation:** Use formal legal rental. Better protection, not much more expensive.

### Your Residency Address

Your rental address becomes:
- Your official residence address (for residence permit)
- Your address for government registration
- Your tax address (if applicable)

Register at municipality within 30 days of moving (required for residence permit).

## Seasonal Housing

Many expats have two approaches:

### Summer in Bodrum, Winter Elsewhere
- Rent summer (May-Sep) in Bodrum
- Travel/rent elsewhere in winter
- Seasonal rentals are easier

### Year-Round Focus
- Commit to 1-2 year lease
- Build community
- Easier finances
