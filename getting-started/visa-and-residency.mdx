# Visa & Residency: Complete Guide

Understanding visa options is your first crucial decision. Turkey offers several paths for expats from the Gulf.

## Your Options at a Glance

| Option | Duration | Cost | Process | Best For |
|--------|----------|------|---------|----------|
| **Tourist Visa** | 90 days | Free | Automatic on entry | Short-term/exploring |
| **Residence Permit (İkamet)** | 1-2 years | ~$30-50 | 15-45 days | Long-term living |
| **Investment Residency** | 3 years renewable | ~$50-100 | 15-45 days | Business owners |
| **Investor Visa** | 3 years renewable | $400,000+ | 3-4 months | Real estate investors ($400k+) |
| **Business Owner** | 1-2 years renewable | ~$50 | 20-30 days | Self-employed/entrepreneurs |

## 1. Tourist Visa (Free 90 Days)

### How It Works

Most expats from Gulf countries get 90-day visa-free entry under Turkish tourist visa scheme.

**Entry Requirements:**
- Valid passport (6+ months validity)
- Return ticket
- Proof of funds (~$50/day)
- No onward visa required

**Process:** Automatic on arrival (nothing to do)

**Cost:** Free

### Extending Tourist Visa

**Option A: Leave & Return**
- Exit Turkey (fly to Greece, Cyprus, Bulgaria)
- Return on same day or later
- Get another 90 days
- Cost: Flight ($30-80 from nearby)
- Commonly done by digital nomads

**Option B: Get Residence Permit Instead**
- More permanent solution
- Recommended if planning 4+ months

<Callout type="warning">
**Important:** Multiple tourist visa stamps (leaving every 90 days) may trigger scrutiny. Turkish authorities can deny entry if they suspect you're abusing the visa. Don't do this repeatedly without getting a residence permit.
</Callout>

## 2. Residence Permit (İKAMET) - Most Common for Expats

This is what most expats get and the path we recommend.

### What Is a Residence Permit?

Turkish residence permit (ikamet tezkeresi) allows you to legally live in Turkey. It's not a visa equivalent; it's local residency registration.

**Duration:** 1-2 years (renewable)

**Cost:** 
- 1-year: ~$24 (USD equivalent)
- 2-year: ~$50
- *Prices vary slightly by region/year*

**Timeline:** 15-45 days after application

### Who Can Get It?

**Designed for:**
- Anyone planning to stay 4+ weeks
- Long-term expats
- Investors
- Business owners
- Foreign spouses of Turkish citizens
- Remote workers

**Eligibility - Generally Open:**
- Valid passport
- Proof of accommodation
- Health insurance (required!)
- Background check (police report from home country)
- Criminal record check

### Step-by-Step Application Process

<Accordion title="Step 1: Gather Documents (1-2 weeks before application)">

**You'll need:**
- Passport (original + 2 copies)
- Residence certificate (from municipality - see Step 2)
- Proof of health insurance letter
- Police report from your home country (not applicable for tourists, but needed for residency declaration)

**For police report:**
- Contact your embassy
- Some countries can do this online
- Usually takes 1-2 weeks
- Cost: $0-50 depending on country

**For health insurance:**
- Get from Turkish insurance company ($15-30/month minimum)
- Get letter of coverage in Turkish
- OR use international insurance (if recognized in Turkey - ask first)

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Step 2: Get Municipality Residence Certificate (3-5 days)">

**Location:** Your local municipal office (Emniyet Müdürlüğü) or neighborhood office (Muhtar)

**Process:**
1. Go to municipality with ID
2. State you want residence certificate
3. Fill out simple form (in Turkish, officer helps)
4. Pay small fee (~$5-10)
5. Receive certificate (usually same day or within few days)

**What You Need:**
- Rental contract or proof of where you're staying
- Passport

**Office Hours:** 9 AM - 5 PM, weekdays (arrive early, 9-10 AM best)

**Pro Tips:**
- Take expat friend or translator if not confident in Turkish
- Go to local (neighborhood) office first, not main police station
- English is rarely spoken, but process is simple
- Many property managers help with this

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Step 3: Health Insurance (1-2 weeks)">

**What's Required:**
- Proof of health insurance valid in Turkey
- Letter showing coverage

**Options:**

**Option A: Turkish Insurance (Cheapest)**
- Cost: $15-30/month
- Providers: Acibadem, Eurocross, Allianz Turkey
- Process: 
  1. Email insurance company from your country
  2. They send you Turkish provider recommendations
  3. Apply with Turkish company (takes 1-2 days)
  4. Get coverage letter immediately
  
**Option B: International Insurance**
- Cost: $50-150+/month
- Providers: Expat-focused insurance companies
- Must verify Turkey coverage accepted
- Process: Apply, get confirmation

**Option C: Government Insurance (SGK)**
- For employees or self-employed paying taxes
- ~$60-100/month
- Covered through employer or business registration

**Recommendation:** Start with inexpensive Turkish insurance ($20-30) for residence permit. Upgrade to international later if desired.

<Callout type="tip">
**Pro tip:** Many insurance companies can issue letter same day by email. You don't need the actual policy card, just the letter.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Step 4: Police Clearance/Background Check (2-4 weeks)">

**What's Required:** Police report from home country

**How to Get It:**
- Contact your country's police/background check service
- Some countries allow online requests
- Some require in-person at police station
- Cost: $0-50 depending on country
- Timeline: 1-14 days typically

**Examples by Country:**
- **USA:** Can get letter from local police department, or use FBI clearance (takes 1-2 weeks)
- **UK:** Get from police check service online (takes 1-2 weeks)
- **Canada:** Take letter from local police or RCMP (takes 1-2 weeks)
- **UAE:** Request from local police/municipality (can be tricky, see notes below)
- **Qatar:** Request from interior ministry or use expat services

**If You're Applying from UAE/Qatar Specifically:**

The process is more involved because you're still in Gulf. Options:
1. Get letter from UAE/Qatar police (possible but varies by emirate/country)
2. Contact your home country embassy for alternative
3. Some expats travel home briefly to get letter in person
4. Professional background check services can help (costs $50-100 more)

**For UAE Residents:**
- Sharjah/Dubai/Abu Dhabi police don't easily give foreigners clearance letters
- Alternative: Get from your home country (email home, have family help)
- Or wait until after you arrive in Turkey (slower but possible)

<Callout type="warning">
**Don't let this stop you:** If you can't easily get police clearance from Gulf (common issue), you can often continue application without it, or get it after arrival. Talk to Turkish immigration office about alternatives.
</Callout>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Step 5: Submit Application (1 day)">

**Where:** Local Migration Office (Göç İdaresi) or 
Police Department (Emniyet Müdürlüğü) - varies by region

**In Bodrum:** Bodrum Emniyet Müdürlüğü (on main police road)

**What You Bring:**
- Passport (original + copy)
- Municipality residence certificate
- Health insurance letter
- Police report (if available; ask about alternatives if not ready)
- Application form (provided at office)

**Process:**
1. Arrive at office (9-11 AM best)
2. Get application form
3. Fill out with help of officer or translator (~15 minutes)
4. Submit documents
5. Get receipt with appointment date for processing
6. Usually get permit 15-45 days later

**Cost:** 
- 1-year: ~$24 (paid at submission)
- 2-year: ~$50
- Payment: Official bank transfer to government account (officer tells you where)

**Pro Tips:**
- Go early before office gets busy
- Bring photocopy of everything (they'll ask)
- Have cash for bank transfer if paying that way
- Ask if English-speaking officer available

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Step 6: Pick Up Residence Permit (Usually 2-6 weeks)">

**Timeline:** 15-45 days after submission

**What Happens:**
- You get notification (some offices call, some you check back)
- Go to same office
- Receive residence permit document
- Takes 5 minutes

**What's Included:** Takes on:
- Your name and passport number
- Turkish address
- Permit validity dates
- Receipt/official stamp

**Valid From:** Issued date to (usually 1 or 2 years later)

</Accordion>

### Typical Timeline & Cost

```
Total Process: 30-60 days
Costs:
- Police clearance: $0-50
- Health insurance: $20-30 (first month minimum)
- Municipality certificate: $5-10
- Residence permit: $24-50
- Translator/helper (optional): $20-50
TOTAL: $75-150 + time
```

### Common Questions About Residence Permit

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="How long is it valid?">
    You choose 1 or 2 years when applying.
    
    Most expats do 2-year for less hassle.
    
    Is renewable indefinitely by reapplying (same process).
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Can I work on residence permit?">
    **Technically:** No, residence permit ≠ work visa
    
    **Reality:** 
    - Remote work from home: Nobody cares, very common
    - Local employment: Need work permit (different process)
    - Freelancing: Grey area (common, but not officially permitted)
    - Business ownership: Need business registration + tax number
    
    **Most expats:** Work remotely without issues
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Do I need to leave Turkey before getting residence permit?">
    No, you can apply while already in Turkey.
    
    Common process:
    1. Arrive on tourist visa
    2. Get accommodation
    3. Apply for residence permit within 90 days
    4. Wait for approval (stay as tourist until approved)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="What if I can't get police report from my country?">
    Talk to Turkish immigration office.
    
    Options:
    - Proceed without it (may be possible)
    - Get it after arrival (longer timeline)
    - Use alternative document from home country
    - Hire lawyer to help navigate (~$50-100)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Can family members get permits?">
    **Spouse:** Yes, same process
    
    **Children:** Yes, same process
    
    **Adult children:** Yes, separate applications
    
    **Parents:** Usually need application per person
    
    Each person needs separate application, health insurance, background check.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## 3. Investment/Investor Visa (For Business Owners)

If you're starting a business or buying property.

### Real Estate Investment

**Option A: Buying Property ($400k+)**
- Properties over $400,000 USD can qualify for citizenship by investment
- Property must be held for 3 years (cannot be sold)
- Includes spouse and children under 18
- Process takes 3-4 months

**Option B: Bank Deposit ($500k+)**
- Deposit $500,000 USD in a Turkish bank
- Must be held for 3 years
- Alternative path to citizenship

**Option C: Government Bonds ($500k+)**
- Invest $500,000 in government bonds
- Must be held for 3 years

**Option B: Starting Business**
- Register business (şirket)
- Get commercial tax number
- Residence permit as "business owner"
- Duration: 1-2 years renewable
- Cost: Business registration ($50-200) + residence permit ($50)

**See also:** [Starting a Business](/work-and-business/starting-business) section

## 4. Residence Card (SOR) - Long-Term Version

After holding residence permit for several years, some expats get:

**Turkish Residence Card (SOR - Statü Oturma Ruhsat)** 
- Long-term residency (3-5 years+)
- Less bureaucratic renewal
- Closer to permanent status without citizenship

**Requirements:** Usually 5+ years holding residence permits

## Citizenship Questions

<Accordion title="Can I get Turkish citizenship?">

**Timeline:** Minimum 5-8 years of continuous residence

**Options:**
1. **Citizenship through residence:** After 5+ years as resident
2. **Citizenship through investment:** Buying $250k+ property
3. **Citizenship through marriage:** Married to Turkish citizen (3 years)

**Process:** Complex, beyond scope here. Consult Turkish lawyer if interested.

**Dual citizenship:** Turkey generally allows dual citizenship, but check with home country rules.

</Accordion>

## Tips from Experienced Expats

<Callout type="success">
**What Works:**

✓ Apply for residence permit early (within 90-day tourist visa)
✓ Connect with other expats who've done this process
✓ Have all documents organized before going to offices
✓ Go to municipal/police offices EARLY (9-11 AM)
✓ Be patient; Turkish bureaucracy is slow but usually works
✓ If stuck, hire a lawyer to help (~$50-100 for whole process)
✓ Keep copies of everything digitally
✓ Get health insurance as soon as possible
</Callout>
