# Taxes & Finance

Understanding Turkish tax system is crucial for legal compliance and financial optimization.

## Turkish Tax System Overview

### Who Needs to File

**Must file if:**
- Working for Turkish employer
- Running Turkish business
- Self-employed with Turkish clients
- Living in Turkey 183+ days/year
- Owning property in Turkey

**May need to file if:**
- Remote worker (recommendation: yes, even if $0 income)
- Just arrived (depends on residency status)

**Don't necessarily need if:**
- Purely foreign income, short-term
- Retired living on foreign pensions
- Consulting: Depends on circumstances

**Real recommendation:** Consult tax professional ($50-100 consultation) on your specific situation

### Tax Brackets (2024-2025)

**Income tax (Gelir Vergisi) - 2024:**
- 0 - 110,000 TRY: 15%
- 110,001 - 230,000 TRY: 20%
- 230,001 - 580,000 TRY: 27%
- 580,001 - 3,000,000 TRY: 35%
- Above 3,000,000 TRY: 40%

**Income tax (Gelir Vergisi) - 2025:**
- 0 - 158,000 TRY: 15%
- 158,001 - 330,000 TRY: 20%
- 330,001 - 800,000 TRY: 27%
- 800,001 - 4,300,000 TRY: 35%
- Above 4,300,000 TRY: 40%

**Corporate Tax Rate (2024-2025):**
- General businesses: 25%
- Financial institutions (banks, insurance): 30%
- New minimum tax rule in 2025: 10% on pre-exemption income

**Reality for expats:**
- Most fall in 15-20% bracket (self-employed)
- Employees have withholding (automatic)
- Self-employed pay estimated quarterly

### VAT (KDV)

**Value-Added Tax (Turkish sales tax):**
- Standard: 18%
- Reduced: 8% and 5% (for certain goods)
- Added on top of prices you see

**If running business:**
- Charge VAT 18% (collect from clients)
- Pay VAT on business purchases
- File monthly/quarterly VAT returns
- Net out (pay difference to government)

**If freelancing/services:**
- Generally exempt from VAT (services to international clients)
- Can still register and reclaim purchases
- Ask accountant about your situation

## Tax Registration Process

### Getting Tax ID (Vergi Numarası)

**Required for:**
- Any business operation
- Self-employment
- Property ownership
- Banking (business accounts)

**Process:**
1. Visit local tax office (Vergi Müdürlüğü) or chambers
2. Bring: Passport, residence proof
3. Fill out application (Turkish, officer helps)
4. Instant ID issuance (usually)
5. Get official document

**Cost:** Free

**Timeline:** 1 day

### KVK Registration (Business Registration)

**If self-employed or business owner:**

**Process:**
1. Go to Commerce Chamber (Ticaret Odası)
2. Bring: Passport, tax ID, business info
3. Register your business
4. Get chamber confirmation
5. Receive KVK number

**Cost:** ~$50-100 (one-time), annual fee ~$20

**Timeline:** 1-2 days

**Benefits:**
- Official business status
- Ability to invoice clients
- Business banking
- Access to business services

## Filing Taxes: Self-Employed/Freelancer

### Annual Return (Yıllık Vergi Beyannamesi)

**What:** Annual income tax return

**Timeline:**
- File by: March 25 following tax year
- Penalties: Late filing has penalties
- Extensions: Available through accountant

**Documents needed:**
- All invoices (issued and received)
- Business expense receipts
- Bank statements
- Proof of payments made
- Medical expenses (if claiming)
- Asset purchases

### Deductible Expenses

**What you can deduct:**
- Office supplies
- Software/subscriptions
- Internet/phone (portion used for business)
- Professional development (courses, books)
- Home office portion (mortgage/rent, utilities)
- Meals with clients
- Travel for business
- Professional fees (accountant, lawyer)
- Equipment (computer, furniture)
- Subscriptions/memberships (professional)

**What you can't deduct:**
- Personal expenses
- Meals and entertainment (limits apply)
- Car (unless business-exclusive)
- Fines/penalties
- Personal vacation

**Keep receipts:** For everything deductible (minimum 3 years)

### Quarterly Estimated Taxes (Stopaj)

**If self-employed earning substantial income:**
- File quarterly estimates
- Due dates: April 20, July 20, October 20, January 20
- Avoid penalties for underpayment
- Calculate based on previous years + current projection

**Who handles:** Usually accountant

**Cost:** Accountant fee ~$20-50 per quarter

### VAT Returns (KDV)

**If registered for VAT:**
- Monthly or quarterly filings (to be determined at registration)
- Report sales + VAT collected
- Report purchases + VAT paid
- Pay net (if collecting more) or request refund (if paid more)
- File on time to avoid penalties

**Many freelancers:** VAT-exempt (ask accountant)

## Tax Filing Methods

### Self-Filing

**If knowledgeable:**
- Download forms from tax office website
- Complete yourself (Turkish needed)
- File in person or online
- Understand deductions/compliance
- Keep meticulous records

**Reality:** Complex. Not recommended unless very comfortable

### Using an Accountant

**Process:**
1. Hire Turkish accountant (muhasebe müşaviri)
2. Provide documents throughout year
3. Accountant prepares return
4. You sign off
5. File together or accountant files

**Cost:**
- Initial consultation: $0-50
- Quarterly (if doing): $50-100 quarterly
- Annual return only: $100-300
- Ongoing bookkeeping: $50-100/month

**Recommendation:** Worth it for peace of mind and legal compliance

### Finding an Accountant

**Where to search:**
- Ask other expats (Facebook groups)
- Chamber of Commerce recommendations
- Online search "Bodrum muhasebe"
- Hotel concierge (often have recommendations)
- Real estate agents (often know accountants)

**Vetting:**
- English-speaking (essential)
- Experience with expats
- References from other clients
- Understands your situation
- Responsive communication

**Cost comparison:** Get quotes from 2-3 before choosing

## Remote Worker Tax Considerations

### Foreign Income

**If working remote for UAE/international employer:**
- Income is foreign-sourced
- Not directly taxable in Turkey by Turkish law
- BUT: Should register and report according to rules

**Recommendation:**
- Register for tax ID (even if not working yet)
- File annual return showing no Turkish income
- Creates documentation trail
- Prevents future legal issues

**Practical reality:** Many remote workers do this, few have issues, but documentation is wise

### Rental Income (If Investing)

**If renting out property:**
- Income IS taxable
- Deduct expenses (mortgage, utilities, maintenance, management)
- File rental income
- 15-20% effective tax rate typical

**Important:** Proper registration makes this legitimate

### cryptocurrency/Crypto Income

**If earning from crypto:**
- Gains ARE taxable
- Track all transactions
- Report on income tax
- Not regulated but reportable
- Keep records

**Reality:** Most don't report, but legally should

|------|---|---|---|
| **Remote Worker** | Report (if registering) | Annual | Light |
| **Self-employed/Freelancer** | Yes, quarterly estimated + annual | Quarterly + Annual | Medium |
| **Turkish Employee** | Automatic withholding | Employer files | None (withhold) |
| **Business Owner** | Yes, business + personal taxes | Various | Heavy |
| **Retiree/Passive Income** | Depends on source | Annual | Light-Medium |

## Social Security (SGK)

### Self-Employed Contributions

**If self-employed earning above threshold:**
- Must register with SGK
- Monthly contribution: ~$80-100
- Provides: Health insurance + retirement benefits
- Quarterly reporting required

**Benefits:**
- Coverage at 80% of costs (patient pays 20%)
- Prescription coverage
- Hospitalization covered
- Dental covered (limited)
- Retirement pension building

### Employee Contributions

**If employed formally:**
- Employer deducts ~13% from salary (withheld)
- Employer pays additional ~16%
- Automatic, you don't manage
- Building retirement benefit

## Financial Planning

### Expat Tax Optimization

**Strategies (legal):**
1. **Business structure:** Proper registration allows deductions
2. **Expense tracking:** Maximize legitimate deductions
3. **Timing:** Year-end planning for deductions
4. **Currency:** Manage foreign exchange strategically
5. **Savings:** Building investment accounts

### International Considerations

**If you have assets/income in multiple countries:**
- FATCA regulations (US citizens especially)
- Foreign bank account reporting
- Worldwide income reporting (Turkey perspective)
- Tax treaties (Turkey has many - check with accountant)

**Reality:** Complex if substantial international assets. Consult specialist.

### Retirement Planning

**Turkey doesn't force retirement accounts like Gulf:**
- Build personal savings
- Invest internationally (if permitted)
- Property investment (growing trend)
- Business building (long-term asset)

**Advantages potential:**
- 15-20% lower taxes
- Lower inflation impact (maintain buying power)
- Property appreciation
- Business equity

## Compliance & Penalties

### Common Violations (and penalties)

| Violation | Penalty | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Late tax filing | 0.5% per day (max 5%) | $25-50 typically |
| Underpayment | Interest ($) + penalties | $100-500 |
| Not filing | 5% of tax + criminal penalty | $500-1000+ |
| False reporting | Criminal prosecution | Serious |

**Reality:** Low enforcement in practice but possible

### Audit Risk

**Risk factors increasing audit probability:**
- Large foreign income deposits
- Inconsistent reporting
- Self-employment with high income
- Large variances year-to-year
- Business that seems unprofitable

**Audit process:**
- Tax office may request records
- You or accountant responds
- Interview possible
- Additional documentation requested
- Negotiation possible
- Penalties if issues found

**Prevention:** Proper record-keeping and accounting
