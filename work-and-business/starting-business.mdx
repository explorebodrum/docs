# Starting a Business in Bodrum

Bodrum offers opportunities for entrepreneurs. Here's how to legally establish and run a business.

## Business Opportunities in Bodrum

### Popular Expat Business Types

**Tourism & Hospitality:**
- Tour operations
- Holiday rental management
- Restaurant/café
- Hotel/accommodation
- Activity centers (yoga, fitness, water sports)

**Services:**
- Real estate agency
- Consulting (business, management, etc.)
- Coaching/online courses
- Web design/digital services
- Translation/language services
- Coaching (wellness, business, personal)

**Retail & E-commerce:**
- Online businesses (operates internationally)
- Coworking spaces
- Gift shop/retail
- Property management

**Creative:**
- Art galleries
- Writing/publishing
- Photography studio
- Music lessons

### Feasibility Factors

**Good prospect if:**
- You understand target market
- Have some startup capital ($1,000-5,000 minimum)
- Willing to accept learning curve
- Fluent in Turkish or hire someone who is
- Have backup income source initially
- Committed to staying 3+ years

**Risky if:**
- Undercapitalized
- No local market knowledge
- No experience in business type
- No Turkish language/connections
- Expecting income in first year
- Planning to set up and leave

## Legal Business Structure

### Sole Proprietorship (Individual)

**Simplest option:**

**Structure:**
- Your personal business
- You are legally responsible
- Personal assets at risk
- No separate entity

**Registration:**
- Just need tax ID + KVK registration
- Cost: ~$50-100
- Timeline: 1-2 days
- Minimal paperwork

**Taxes:**
- Self-employed taxes (15-20%)
- SGK registration required (if above income)
- File personal tax return (business income included)

**Best for:**
- Freelancers
- Consultants
- Solo operations
- Low-liability businesses

**Reality:** Most expat businesses start this way

---

### Company (Limited Liability - Ltd - Şirketi)

**More formal structure:**

**Structure:**
- Separate legal entity
- Limited liability (personal assets protected)
- Can have multiple owners/shareholders
- More formal accounting

**Registration:**
- Register at Commerce Chamber
- Create founding documents
- Specific legal requirements
- Cost: $200-500
- Timeline: 3-5 days
- More bureaucracy

**Capitalization:**
- **Minimum capital required: 50,000 TRY** (approx. $1,500-2,000 USD) as of 2024-2025
- Must be deposited in business account within 24 months of registration
- No upfront deposit required for LTD
- Capital can be contributed in cash or in kind
- Each share must have a nominal value of at least 25 TRY

**Taxes:**
- **Corporate tax rate: 25%** (2024-2025)
- Financial institutions (banks, insurance): 30%
- Must file company taxes separately
- Employees require formal contracts
- More accounting complexity
- New minimum tax rule in 2025: 10% on pre-exemption income

**Best for:**
- Multiple owners/partners
- Seeking investor funding
- Hiring employees
- Substantial operations

**Reality:** Overkill for most small expat businesses

---

### Partnership

**Two or more owners:**

**Benefits:**
- Shared responsibility
- Shared capital
- Income splitting

**Challenges:**
- Partnership disputes (common)
- Legal complexity
- Both liable unless Ltd.

**Reality:** Many partnerships fail. Get attorney if considering.

**Recommendation:** If partnering, hire lawyer to create clear partnership agreement ($300-500 worth of prevention)

## Setting Up Your Business

### Step 1: Business Planning

**Essentials:**
- Business idea validated (test market first?)
- Target market identified
- Initial budget ($2,000-10,000 typical for small business)
- Revenue model clear
- Operating plan for first year
- 3-year projection realistic

**Reality:** Many skip formal plan. At minimum, think through above.

### Step 2: Registering Your Business

**Process:**

1. **Choose business name**
   - Check availability at Commerce Chamber
   - Not trademarked
   - Turkish-appropriate
   - Cost: Free check

2. **Go to Commerce Chamber (Ticaret Odası)**
   - Bodrum has one
   - Bring: Passport, residence proof
   - Fill out registration paperwork
   - Pay registration fee (~$50-100)
   - Get KVK certificate (24 hours to few days)

3. **Get Tax ID**
   - Visit tax office with KVK certificate
   - Fill out form
   - Get Vergi Numarası (instant usually)

4. **Open Business Bank Account**
   - Visit bank with documents
   - KVK + Tax ID
   - Bring: Passport, residence proof
   - Deposit initial capital (if company, must deposit minimum)
   - Get business account + card

5. **Register with SGK (if needed)**
   - If self-employed over threshold
   - Go to SGK office
   - Register for health insurance + retirement
   - Monthly ~$80-100
   - Quarterly filings

### Step 3: Getting Started

**Initial setup:**
- Find location (if physical business)
- Get insurance
- Set up accounting system
- Create business cards/website
- Launch marketing

## Business Licenses & Permits

### General License

**Required for:**
- All businesses generally need
- Issued by municipality
- "Ticari Faaliyet Ruhsatı"

**Process:**
- Go to municipality (Belediye)
- Provide: KVK certificate, tax ID, business details
- Fill out application
- Pay fee (~$50-150)
- Get license

**Valid:** Usually 1 year, renewed annually

### Specific Licenses

Depending on business type, you may need:

| Business Type | License Required | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Restaurant/Café** | Food service license | Health inspection required |
| **Tourism** | Tourism authority registration | Required for tour operations |
| **Real Estate** | Chamber membership | Some requirements |
| **Car Rental** | Special permits | Insurance requirements |
| **Accommodation** | Municipal license | Safety standards |
| **Retail** | General license | Usually sufficient |
| **Consulting** | Generally none specific | Just general license |
| **Teaching** | No license (just register) | If self-employed teacher |

**General rule:** Ask municipality or lawyer what's needed for your specific business

## Business Insurance

### Types Needed

**General Liability:**
- Covers accidents/injuries
- Covers property damage
- Cost: $100-500/year
- Essential for any business

**Professional Liability:**
- If providing services
- Covers malpractice/errors
- Cost: $100-300/year
- Ask insurance company if needed

**Property Insurance:**
- If owning equipment/stock
- If renting commercial space
- Cost: Varies by property value

**Vehicle:**
- If using car for business
- Commercial insurance rate (higher)
- Required if business car

**Where to Buy:**
- Insurance companies directly
- Insurance broker (can compare)
- Expat groups have recommendations

## Banking for Business

### Business Bank Account

**Essential for:**
- Separation of personal/business finances
- Invoicing/professional appearance
- Accounting records
- Tax reporting

**What you need:**
- KVK certificate
- Tax ID
- Passport
- Residence proof
- Initial deposit (varies by bank)

**Cost:**
- Monthly fee: $5-15
- Transaction fees: Minimal with business account
- First month: Usually free

**Recommended:**
- Garanti or İşbank (best service for business accounts)
- English-speaking support
- Good online banking

### International Transfers

**You'll need to transfer money:**
- Sending to suppliers
- Receiving payment from international clients
- Bringing in startup capital
- Sending profits home

**Options:**
- Turkish bank wire: $15-25 per transfer
- Wise: 1-2% fee (best for regular transfers)
- Crypto: Not recommended for business

## Hiring Employees

### Legal Requirements

**If hiring:**
- Employee contracts required (Turkish legal format)
- SGK registration mandatory
- Salary withholdings for taxes
- Legal benefits documentation
- Workplace safety compliance

**Costs:**
- Salary 200%+ (employer contributes matching)
- SGK: ~16% additional
- Administrative overhead

**Example:** $500 employee salary = $580-600 employer cost

### Employment Contracts

**Must include:**
- Position and responsibilities
- Salary amount
- Work hours
- Benefits
- Probation period (2-3 months typical)
- Termination terms
- Turkish law provisions

**Requirement:** Use Turkish legal contract (not English one)

**Lawyer cost:** $100-200 to create proper contract

### Hiring Process

**Reality:**
- Labor laws protect employees strongly  
- Termination requires just cause
- Minimum severance
- Paid vacation (14 days required)

**Recommendation:** Understand labor laws before hiring. Most small businesses don't need them immediately.

## Accounting & Record-Keeping

### Essential Records

**Keep for 3 years minimum:**
- All invoices (issued and received)
- Bank statements
- Expense receipts
- Income records
- Tax payments
- Employee records (if applicable)

**Organization:**
- Monthly folders
- Digital + physical backups
- Accounting software (Wave, Zoho, etc.)
- Easy audit trail

### Annual Accounting

**Required:**
- Income statement (profit/loss)
- Balance sheet (assets/liabilities)
- Tax calculations
- Filed with tax office by March 25

**Cost:**
- Accountant: $200-500 annually
- Self-filed: Free but complex

**Highly recommended:** Use accountant for proper compliance

## Common Business Mistakes

**Avoid these:**

1. **Undercapitalization:** Start with at least $2,000-5,000
2. **Poor record-keeping:** Organize from day one
3. **No business plan:** Think through model first
4. **Ignoring taxes:** File properly
5. **Poor partnerships:** Get legal agreement
6. **Hiring too early:** Bootstrap as long as possible
7. **Overestimating market:** Test first, scale after
8. **Ignoring competition:** Know your market
9. **No contingency:** Plan for slow times
10. **Hiring wrong people:** Culture fit matters more than you think

## Business Visa/Residency

**Important:** Business ownership makes it easier to maintain residency

**Benefits:**
- Clearly legitimate reason to be in Turkey
- Easier to demonstrate financial stability
- Can sponsor dependents
- Path to longer-term residency

**Not required:** Residence permit sufficient for most businesses
