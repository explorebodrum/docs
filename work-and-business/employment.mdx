## Employment Contract & Conditions

### Turkish Employment Law

**Key terms:**
- **Min wage:** ~$400/month (varies)
- **Work week:** 45 hours standard (can negotiate)
- **Vacation:** 14 days minimum annually (law)
- **Notice:** 2 weeks typical
- **Probation:** 2-3 months common
- **Taxes:** Withheld by employer

### Contract Elements

**Important to clarify:**
- Salary amount (in Turkish Lira, typically)
- Payment frequency (monthly)
- Vacation days
- Benefits (health insurance - SGK)
- Overtime/bonuses
- Non-compete clause (Turkey has these)
- Termination terms
- Language (insist on English translation)

### Benefits Typically Provided

**If employed formally:**
- SGK health insurance (by law)
- Paid vacation (14 days minimum)
- Holiday bonuses (Turkish tradition)
- Severance pay (if terminated)

**Not typical:**
- 401k/retirement matching (not Turkish system)
- Stock options (unlikely)
- Flexible work arrangements (improving)
- Remote work options (less common)

## Work Permits

### Requirement

**You need work permit if:**
- Employed by Turkish company/employer
- Self-employed earning mainly Turkish income
- Business owner (employer)

**You don't need if:**
- Remote work for foreign company (grey area)
- Earning foreign currency remotely
- Freelancing internationally

### Work Permit Process

**Who applies:** Employer (not you)

**Process:**
1. Employer hires you formally
2. Employer submits work permit application to labor ministry
3. You provide: Documents (passport, police clearance, CV)
4. Labor ministry reviews (15-30 days)
5. Approval (usually if employer serious)
6. Work permit issued

**Cost:** Employer usually covers

**Documentation needed:**
- Valid passport
- Police clearance (from home country)
- Certificate/credentials (relevant to job)
- CV
- Employment contract

### Residence Card for Work

**After work permit approved:**
- Easier to get residence permit
- Residence card (SOR) may be available
- Long-term stability indicated

**Timeline:** Work permit first, residence permit second

## Teaching English

Most common employment for expats:

### TEFL/TESOL Certificate

**What is it?** Teaching English as Foreign Language certification

**Cost:** $400-2,000 depending on program
- Online: $400-800 (1-4 weeks)
- In-person: $800-2,000 (2-4 weeks)
- Better programs: $1,500-2,000

**Recognized providers:**
- Cambridge TESOL
- Trinity TESOL
- TEFL International
- Online services (varying quality)

**Reality:** 120-hour minimum (cheaper) to 150+ hours (better)

**Job impact:**
- Highly preferred by schools
- Required by international schools
- Helps with salary negotiation
- Increases job opportunities

### Teaching Positions

**International Schools:**
- **Position:** Teacher (English or other subjects)
- **Salary:** $1,500-3,000/month
- **Requirements:** University degree + TEFL + experience
- **Hours:** Full-time, regular school hours
- **Benefits:** Best benefits, SGK, stability
- **Challenges:** Competitive, need credentials

**Language Centers:**
- **Position:** English instructor
- **Salary:** $500-1,000/month
- **Requirements:** TEFL minimum
- **Hours:** Part-time or full-time
- **Benefits:** Flexible, can work multiple centers
- **Challenges:** Lower pay, seasonal variation
- **Number:** $15-20/hour typical

**Private Tutoring:**
- **Position:** Freelance tutor
- **Salary:** $15-30/hour
- **Requirements:** English proficiency
- **Hours:** You set
- **Benefits:** Flexibility, higher hourly rate
- **Challenges:** Finding clients, variable income
- **Path:** Build clientele through expat networks

**Online Teaching:**
- **Position:** Remote English teacher (for international platforms)
- **Salary:** $12-25/hour
- **Requirements:** English native or fluent + computer
- **Hours:** Flexible, can work part-time
- **Companies:** VIPKid, Preply, Chegg others
- **Challenges:** Competition, platform take % of earnings

### Getting Hired to Teach

**Process:**
1. Get TEFL certificate
2. Apply to schools (LinkedIn, school websites)
3. Submit CV + certifications
4. Interview (video or in-person)
5. Background check
6. Start date negotiated
7. Work permit application (if employer-sponsored)

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks typically

**Salary negotiation:**
- Market rate research (ask expats)
- Turkish salaries lower than Gulf
- Expect $1,500-2,500/month for good school position
- Negotiable if experienced

## Seasonal Work

Bodrum's tourism creates seasonal opportunities:

**Summer months (Jun-Aug):**
- Hotels hiring additional staff
- Tour companies busiest
- Higher positions available
- More casual work possible

**Off-season:**
- Layoffs happen
- Fewer opportunities
- Harder hiring environment

**Strategic timing:** Look for jobs Dec-Mar (summer prep)

## Working Hours & Culture

### Turkish Business Hours

**Standard:**
- 9 AM - 1 PM morning
- 1-4 PM break (many places close)
- 4-6 PM evening
- Mon-Fri typical

**Schools:**
- 8 AM-4 PM (long day)
- 15-minute breaks
- Lunch break

### Work Culture

**Differences from Gulf:**
- Less hierarchy (though exists)
- Relationships matter greatly
- Negotiation common
- Slightly more flexible
- Smaller companies = more flexible

**Communication:**
- Turkish business etiquette important
- Learning Turkish shows respect
- English increasingly used
- Professional dress common

## Legal Responsibilities

Employer's job to handle, but understand:

**SGK (Social Insurance):**
- Employer deducts ~13% from salary
- Covers health insurance, retirement
- Mandatory by law
- Employer contributes ~16% additional

**Income tax with holding:**
- 15-20% tax bracket typical
- Deducted from salary
- Annual reconciliation file

**Starting business registration:**
- If you're the employer/owneropez
- Tax ID required
- Business registration required
