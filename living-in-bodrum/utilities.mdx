# Utilities & Internet: Setting Up Your Home

Getting utilities established is one of your first priorities. Here's what you need to know.

## Electricity (Elektrik)

### Setting Up Your Account

**Provider:** TEDAŞ (Turkish state electric company)

**Process:**
1. Go to local electricity office (TEDAŞ branch)
2. Bring: Rental contract, ID, meter number (from apartment)
3. Fill out application (Turkish, officer helps)
4. Provide reading of meter
5. Pay initial deposit
6. Account established (1-3 days)

**Cost:**
- Deposit: Varies ($50-200 based on usage estimate)
- Monthly bill: Varies significantly by usage

### Electricity Costs

**Average usage (3-room apartment):**
- Summer (AC running): $80-150/month
- Winter (mild): $30-50/month
- Spring/Fall: $40-70/month

**Factors:**
- AC is biggest cost (summer can be 60-70% of bill)
- Water heater: Electric heaters use significant power
- Direct/indirect heating affects greatly

<Callout type="warning">
**Budget Planning:** Summer costs can be shocking if not prepared. Plan for $80-150/month AC running continuously.
</Callout>

### Saving on Electricity

**Tips:**
- Use AC selectively (afternoons only vs. 24/7)
- Close shutters during hot hours (traditional method works)
- Air drying clothes (no dryer)
- LED bulbs (low power)
- Unplug devices when not used
- Water heater timer helps

**Reality:** Many expats adjust by accepting room temperatures at 25-26°C instead of 21°C

### Payment

**Methods:**
1. Monthly automatic bank transfer (set up with bank)
2. In-person at TEDAŞ office
3. Online through TEDAŞ website
4. Various bill payment centers

**Billing:** Monthly or bi-monthly (varies by location)

## Water (Su)

### Setting Up

**Provider:** Municipal water authority

**Similar process to electricity:**
1. Rental address + ID
2. Registration at municipal office or TEDAŞ office
3. Minimal setup
4. Takes 1-2 days

**Cost:**
- Deposit: Minimal ($20-50)
- Monthly bill: $10-20 typical (varies by usage)

### Water Quality

**Safety:** Tap water safe to drink in Bodrum
- Chlorinated
- Regular testing
- Infrastructure decent

**Reality:** Many people prefer filtered water (taste preference)

### Installing Filters

**Options:**
- Water pitcher filters: $15-30 (replace cartridges monthly)
- Under-sink reverse osmosis: $100-300 installed
- Whole-house systems: Rare

**Common:** Many expats use pitcher filters for peace of mind

### Water Pressure & Supply Issues

**Potential in Bodrum:**
- Summer water rationing (in severe drought years)
- Pressure fluctuations (especially upper floors)

**Solutions:**
- Water tank installation helps (pressure relief)
- Some apartments have backup tanks
- Ask landlord about setup

**Reality:** Most expats don't encounter major issues

## Gas (Doğalgaz)

### Availability

**Natural gas:** Available in main areas
**Not available:** Some rural/distant neighborhoods (use portable tanks)

### Setting Up

**If available in your area:**
1. Contact gas company (Botas)
2. Apartment must be equipped (check with landlord)
3. Safety inspection required
4. Account opened
5. Monthly billing

**Cost:**
- Deposit: $50-100
- Monthly bill (winter): $30-60
- Summer: Minimal-$10

### Alternatives

**If gas unavailable:**
- Electric water heaters (common)
- Portable gas bottles (propane tanks)
- Wood/solid fuel (rare for expats)

**Most common:** Electric water heaters

## Internet (İnternet)

### Critical Decision

**Most important utility for expats** - especially remote workers

**Test speed BEFORE signing lease** if you work online

### Internet Providers

| Provider | Coverage | Speed | Reliability | Cost |
|----------|----------|-------|---|---|
| **Turk Telecom (TTNet)** | Excellent | 50-100 Mbps | Very good | $20-40 |
| **Vodafone** | Good | 50-100 Mbps | Good | $25-40 |
| **Superonline** | Good | 50-100 Mbps | Good | $25-40 |
| **Local providers** | Variable | Variable | Variable | $20-35 |

**Recommendation:** Turk Telecom most reliable in Bodrum

### Internet Types

**Fiber (Fiber optik)**
- Best option: 100+ Mbps
- Increasingly available
- Cost: $25-40/month
- Reliability: Excellent
- Speed: Consistent

**ADSL (Copper lines)**
- Older technology
- Speed: 15-50 Mbps
- Becoming less common
- Cost: $20-30/month
- Reliability: Good

**Wireless (Kablosuz)**
- For areas without fiber/ADSL
- Speed: Variable
- Reliability: Moderate
- Cost: $20-35/month

**Telephone lines:** Needed for ADSL (building must have TTNet telephone infrastructure)

### Getting Internet Installed

**Process:**
1. Contact provider
2. Technical survey (if fiber needed)
3. Determine feasibility
4. Schedule installation
5. Install box/modem
6. Account activated
7. Takes 3-14 days depending

**Cost:**
- Installation: $0-50
- Modem: Included or $10-30
- Monthly: $20-40

### Internet Speed Reality

**Testing:**
- Speedtest.net (check before signing)
- Most providers deliver promised speeds
- Peak hours (evening) can slow down
- Download vs upload speeds vary

**Typical speeds:**
- Download: 50-100 Mbps
- Upload: 10-20 Mbps
- Ping: 10-30 ms

**For remote work:**
- Minimum 10 Mbps download (surfing)
- Better 25+ Mbps (video calls)
- Solid 50+ Mbps (large files/multiple users)
- Fiber (100+ Mbps) ideal

### Backup Internet

**Recommendation for remote workers:**
- Primary: Home fiber internet
- Backup: Mobile hotspot (Turkish phone SIM)

**Turkish mobile data:**
- Cost: $10-20/month for 10-20GB
- Providers: Vodafone, Turkcell, Türk Telecom
- Reliable: Very (better than home internet sometimes)
- Activation: Immediate from local shop

<Callout type="tip">
**Pro Tip:** If you work remotely, get both home internet + mobile SIM for backup. Mobile data in Turkey is cheap insurance against internet outages.
</Callout>

### WiFi Quality

**Router:**
- ISP typically provides decent router
- Can upgrade ($50-100 for good one)
- WiFi range: Usually covers apartment
- 2.4GHz + 5GHz bands provided by newer routers

**Speed on WiFi:**
- Usually 60-80% of wired speed
- Wired connection faster (ethernet cable)
- For critical work: Use wired connection

## Phone (Telefon)

### Getting a SIM Card

**Immediate priority** when you arrive

**Providers:**
1. **Turkcell** - Most popular
2. **Vodafone** - Good coverage
3. **Türk Telecom** - Also reliable

**To get SIM:**
- Visit any provider shop (throughout Bodrum)
- Bring: Passport
- Pay: $10-20 (prepaid) or $20-40 (contract)
- Activated: Immediately

### Phone Plans

**Prepaid (Kontrol):**
- No contract
- Top-up as needed
- Usually 1-3 month validity
- Cost: $10-30 per month equivalent
- No commitment

**Contract (Tarifeli):**
- Monthly plan
- 12-month commitment
- Usually cheaper
- Data/talk/text included
- Cost: $20-40/month typical

### Typical Plans

**Budget ($15-20/month):**
- Limited data (3-5GB)
- Unlimited domestic talk/text
- Good for light use

**Standard ($25-35/month):**
- 10-15GB data
- Unlimited domestic
- Some international minutes
- Good for most

**Premium ($40-50/month):**
- 25-30GB data
- Unlimited all
- International options
- Good for heavy use

### International Calls

**To home country:**
- Cost: $0.30-1.00 per minute
- Apps cheaper: WhatsApp calls (data-based)
- Packages available: Often cheaper rates

**Getting called:**
- Turkish number receives SMS/calls normally
- International calling: Expensive for callers

### Using Turkish Phone While Abroad

**Within Turkey:** All data/calls work

**In Greece (day trip):** Sometimes connects to Greek network (higher fees apply)

**Elsewhere:** Roaming charges (expensive unless plan includes)

## Internet for remote workers: Special Considerations

<Callout type="success">
**Bodrum for Remote Work:**

✓ Internet generally reliable 50-100 Mbps
✓ Fiber increasingly available
✓ Backup mobile internet available
✓ Cost very low ($20-40/month)
✓ Good time zones for Europe

⚠ Peak hours (evening) can slow slightly
⚠ Power outages rare but happen
⚠ ISP support in Turkish (challenging)
</Callout>

**Recommendations:**
- Test internet BEFORE renting apartment
- Fiber areas better for reliability
- Get mobile backup SIM
- Consider UPS/power backup
- Have tech support contact in home country
- Coworking space backup ($50-150/month) possible

## Troubleshooting Issues

**Internet slow:** 
- Restart router (often fixes)
- Check for interference
- Test wired connection
- Contact ISP if persistent

**Power outages:**
- Rare, but can happen
- UPS helps for computer/router
- Call to report
- Check on neighbors (share transformer)

**Water issues:**
- Low pressure: Might need tank
- Discoloration: Usually temporary
- Contact water company

**Gas smell:**
- Immediate action: Open windows
- Do NOT use electrical devices
- Call gas company emergency
- Evacuate if strong
