# Yachting & Boating in Bodrum

Bodrum is one of Turkey's premier yachting destinations, offering world-class marina facilities, a vibrant sailing culture, and access to some of the Aegean Sea's most beautiful waters. Whether you're looking to charter a yacht, own a vessel, or simply enjoy maritime activities, Bodrum has everything you need.

### Yalikavak Palmarina - The Crown Jewel

Considered one of the Mediterranean's finest marinas:

**Facilities:**
- 694 berths accommodating yachts up to 100+ meters
- Full-service maintenance and repair facilities
- Luxury boutiques and restaurants
- Crew facilities including gym and relaxation areas
- 24/7 security and concierge services

**Costs:**
- Average: €150-300/night for mid-size yachts
- Luxury superyacht berths available

<Callout type="info">
**Pro Tip:** Yalikavak Palmarina attracts an international clientele and hosts prestigious yachting events. It's the social hub for yacht owners in Bodrum.
</Callout>

## Boat Ownership in Bodrum

### Costs of Owning a Boat/Yacht

| Expense | Small Boat (8-12m) | Mid-Size (12-20m) | Large Yacht (20m+) |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Purchase Price** | €50,000-200,000 | €200,000-1M | €1M+ |
| **Annual Marina Fees** | €3,000-8,000 | €15,000-50,000 | €50,000+ |
| **Insurance** | €1,500-5,000 | €5,000-20,000 | €20,000+ |
| **Maintenance** | €2,000-5,000 | €10,000-30,000 | €30,000+ |
| **Crew Costs** | Optional | €20,000-40,000/year | €50,000+/year |

### Licensing Requirements

**For Turkish Waters:**
- Recreational boat license required for vessels over 10 gross tons
- International Certificate of Competence (ICC) accepted
- Skipper courses available locally

**For Charter Operations:**
- Commercial licensing required
- Safety certifications mandatory
- Crew certification standards

<Callout type="tip">
**Tax Advantages:** Turkey offers attractive yacht ownership tax structures compared to EU countries. Consult with a maritime lawyer for specifics.
</Callout>

## Greek Island Hopping

One of Bodrum's great advantages is proximity to Greek islands:

### Popular Destinations

| Island | Distance | Ferry Duration | Character |
|--------|----------|---------------|-----------|
| **Symi** | 25 nm | 1.5 hours | Colorful harbor, monastery |
| **Rhodes** | 40 nm | 2 hours | Medieval town, beaches |
| **Tilos** | 35 nm | 2 hours | Quiet, unspoiled |
| **Kos** | 20 nm | 1 hour | Beaches, ancient sites |
| **Nisyros** | 30 nm | 1.5 hours | Volcanic island |

### Practical Information

- **Ferry Services:** Regular services from Bodrum and Turgutreis
- **Day Trips:** Available through charter services
- **Costs:** €30-80 per person for ferries
- **Visa:** Greek visa not required for Turkish citizens; Schengen rules apply for others

<Callout type="info">
**Pro Tip:** The best time for island hopping is May-June and September-October when seas are calm and tourist crowds are smaller.
</Callout>

## Getting Started as an Expat

### For Yacht Enthusiasts

If you're passionate about boating:

1. **Join the community:** Bodrum Yacht Club welcomes members
2. **Start with charters:** Experience different yacht types before buying
3. **Take courses:** Local sailing schools offer certifications
4. **Network:** Marina bars and restaurants are social hubs
5. **Consider membership:** Yacht clubs offer reciprocal privileges

### Investment Opportunities

**Charter Management Programs:**
- Purchase a yacht and let a management company rent it
- Typically 8-12% return on investment
- Usage rights for personal use

**Marina Investments:**
- Berths can be purchased as investments
- Rental income when not in use
