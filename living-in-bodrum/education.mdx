# Education Options in Bodrum

If you're moving with children, education is crucial. Bodrum offers several options.

## Educational System Overview

### International Schools

Best option for expats preferring curriculum continuity:

**International School of Bodrum (ISB)** - Main option
- **Curriculum:** English-based (IB program)
- **Ages:** Preschool through Grade 12
- **Language:** English (with Turkish/other languages)
- **Accreditation:** Internationally recognized
- **Cost:** $3,500-8,000/year (varies by grade)
- **Location:** Central Bodrum, easily accessible
- **Community:** Expat-majority, diverse students
- **Reputation:** Well-established, good outcomes

**Other International/Bilingual Schools:**
- Bodrum Bilingual School
- Various smaller international options
- Prices: $2,500-6,000/year

### Turkish National Schools

Educational benefit but challenges for expats:

**Public Schools (Devlet Okulları)**
- **Curriculum:** Full Turkish curriculum
- **Language:** Turkish (English as subject)
- **Cost:** Free (though small fees)
- **Quality:** Variable, better in cities
- **Integration:** Best for cultural immersion
- **Challenge:** Language barrier, curriculum different

**Private Turkish Schools**
- **Curriculum:** Turkish curriculum, English emphasis
- **Cost:** $1,500-4,000/year
- **Quality:** Often excellent
- **Language:** Turkish primary, strong English
- **Good option:** Balance of Turkish + English

### Turkish High Schools

**Lycée system (Lise):**
- Ages 14-18
- Exam-based advancement
- University prep focused
- Turkish curriculum
- Private options with English emphasis

## Choosing a School

### Decision Factors

| Factor | International | Turkish Private | Turkish Public |
|--------|---|---|---|
| **Language** | English | Mixed | Turkish |
| **Curriculum** | International | Turkish+English | Turkish |
| **Integration** | Limited | Medium | High |
| **Cost** | High | Medium | Low-free |
| **Social** | Expat-focused | Mixed | Turkish |
| **Continuity** | Easy to transfer | Harder | Need language |

### Questions to Ask

1. **Curriculum:** What system? (IB, British, American, Turkish?)
2. **Language instruction:** How much Turkish/English?
3. **Accreditation:** Recognized where? (important if moving)
4. **Class size:** How many students per class?
5. **Facilities:** Laboratories, sports, arts programs?
6. **Transportation:** School buses available?
7. **Extracurriculars:** Sports teams, clubs, activities?
8. **Parent involvement:** Expected level?
9. **Diversity:** Student body mix?
10. **Costs:** Any hidden fees?

## International School Details

### International School of Bodrum (ISB)

**Strengths:**
✓ Well-established (20+ years)
✓ IB program (recognized globally)
✓ Excellent facilities
✓ Sports programs & teams
✓ Arts & music
✓ University counseling
✓ Diverse student body
✓ Good reputation with universities

**Weaknesses:**
✗ Most expensive option
✗ Not much Turkish culture exposure
✗ Expat bubble (limited integration)
✗ Enrollment filling (competitive)
✗ Summer schedule affects local socializing

**Cost:**
- Preschool: $3,500-4,500/year
- Elementary: $4,500-5,500/year
- Middle: $5,500-7,000/year
- High: $7,000-8,000/year
- Additional: Entry fees, books, etc.

**Enrollment:**
- Rolling admissions, but filling quickly
- Application process standard (entrance tests sometimes)
- Spaces competitive in popular grades

**Contacting:**
- Website: Search "ISB Bodrum" or ask expats
- Campus visit: Recommended
- Current families: Great resource via Facebook

### Other International Options

Several smaller schools exist, quality/cost variable:

**Turkish Schools with English Emphasis:**
- Bodrum Bilingual School
- Various private schools
- Costs: $2,500-5,000/year
- Mixed curriculum approaches

**Homeschooling Option:**
- Some expat families homeschool
- Online international schools available
- ISS, Connections Academy, others available
- Cost: $5,000-12,000/year
- Requires parent commitment

## Turkish School System (If Considering)

### Structure

**Primary (İlkokul):** Ages 6-10 (4 years)
**Secondary (Ortaokul):** Ages 10-14 (4 years)
**High (Lise):** Ages 14-18 (4 years)

### Entrance Tests

**High School:** National exam (very competitive)
**University:** National entrance exam (critical)

**Reality for expats:** Can navigate but requires Turkish fluency

### Turkish Language

**Essential for Turkish schools:**
- Limited English instruction
- Usually taught as subject only
- Textbooks primarily Turkish
- Classmates speak Turkish

**Learning Turkish:**
- Private tutoring available
- Language school options
- Immersion approach (children learn):
- Takes 1-2 years for academic fluency

### Benefits of Turkish Schools

- Cultural integration
- Turkish language learning
- Recognized education system
- Good universities connections
- Lower cost
- Local community access

### Challenges

- Language barrier initially
- Different curriculum
- Testing different from Western
- Less international recognition
- Cultural adjustment

## University Preparation

### Turkish Universities

**If considering Turkey higher ed:**
- University entrance exam (very competitive)
- Acceptance based primarily on entrance score
- Turkish universities quality variable
- English-medium programs increasingly available

### International Options

**Most expat families:**
- Plan for university in home country
- Or Western universities elsewhere
- Keep curriculum aligned (IB helps transition)

**University counseling:**
- International schools provide
- Guides toward UK, US, Canada universities
- Application support
- Standardized testing prep (SAT, ACT, IB exams)

## Practical School Information

### School Calendar

**Turkish schools:** September-June (summer break Jul-Aug)
**International schools:** Often similar, some variations

**Holiday alignment:**
- Not aligned with home country
- Plan summer trips accordingly
- Consider cost implications

### Transportation

**School buses:**
- Available at some schools
- Extra cost ($50-150/month)
- Fixed routes from neighborhoods
- Alternative: Parent carpools

**Independent:**
- Children walking/biking
- Depends on area & age
- Safe in most neighborhoods

### Sports & Activities

**Typically offered:**
- Football (soccer)
- Volleyball
- Basketball
- Swimming
- Tennis
- Various clubs (debate, arts, etc.)

**Cost:** Usually included or small added fee

## Special Education & Needs

**Services available:**
- International schools have more support
- Turkish schools less comprehensive
- Private tutoring option
- English-speaking specialists rare

**If special needs:** International school likely better option

## First Move Coordination

### Timing

**School year starts:** Usually September

**Best move timing:**
- Late August (settle before school)
- Or late June-August (summer transition)
- Mid-year entry: Possible but harder
- Visiting May-June: Check schools before committing

### Documentation Needed

**For enrollment:**
- Birth certificate + translation
- Previous school transcripts
- Medical records (vaccinations)
- Proof of address
- Passport

**Translation:** Get certified Turkish translations

### Trial Options

**Before committing:**
- Summer programs (some available)
- Short-term rentals (explore area)
- School visits (arrange before moving)
- Talk to other expat families (Facebook)
