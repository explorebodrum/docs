# Living in Bodrum: Your Guide

You've arrived and settled in. Now it's time to build your life. This section covers everything from neighborhoods to daily living.

## Key Decisions to Make

### Neighborhood Choice

Your first decision—where to actually live? This shapes your whole experience.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Social & Active">

## Daily Life Rhythms

### Understanding Turkish Time

Turkish daily schedule is different from Gulf:

**Morning (6-9 AM)**
- Early risers common
- Breakfast is substantial
- Offices open 9 AM
- Shops opening

**Mid-Morning (9 AM - 12 PM)**
- Work hours
- Most business conducted
- Coffee culture (breaks)
- Government offices busy

**Afternoon (12 PM - 4 PM)**
- **Lunch break:** 1-2 hours (many businesses close)
- **Siesta time:** Many rest/nap
- Slowest part of day
- Restarting around 4 PM

**Evening (4-8 PM)**
- Second work wave
- Socializing begins
- Beach/promenade time
- Shops reopening/extending hours

**Night (8 PM+)**
- Dinner time (peak restaurant time)
- Social activities
- Nightlife starts 10 PM+
- Beds 11 PM - midnight typical

<Callout type="tip">
**Expat Tip:** This rhythm takes adjustment. You won't get things done at 2 PM. Plan accordingly—morning for errands, evening for social activities.
</Callout>

### Shopping & Markets

**Weekly Farmers Market**
- Saturdays typically in Bodrum
- Fresh produce, excellent prices
- Turkish experience, atmospheric
- 8 AM - 1 PM best time
- Expect haggling for some items

**Supermarkets**
- Carrefour: Main chain, European quality
- Migros: Local chain
- Sok: Budget supermarket
- Hours: Usually 9 AM - 9 PM

**Specialty Shopping**
- Most things available somewhere
- Turkish brands excellent quality
- International brands cost more
- Shopping focused in central areas

## Monthly Rhythms

### Bills & Financial

**Utilities billing:** Monthly or bi-monthly
- Electricity: Most important to budget for
- Water: Usually cheap
- Internet: $20-40/month
- Pay by bank transfer or in person

**Rent:** Monthly by agreed bank transfer

**Insurance:** Monthly automatic (if set up)

### Social Calendar

**Monthly Rhythms:**
- **First week:** New month social reset
- **Mid-month:** Peak tourism (summer)
- **End of month:** Seasonal celebrations
- **Full moon:** Beach parties sometimes
- **Holiday weeks:** Turkish holidays marked

### Seasonal Changes

**Spring (Apr-May)**
- Sea opening for swimming
- Beaches filling with visitors
- Prices starting up
- Outdoor season activated

**Summer (Jun-Aug)**
- Peak everything
- Crowded, expensive
- Nightlife intense
- Work slows (summer mentality)

**Fall (Sep-Oct)**
- School starting (if have kids)
- Tourist season ending
- Prices dropping
- Best weather

**Winter (Nov-Mar)**
- Slower pace
- Local life front and center
- Rainy sometimes
- Lowest prices
- Best for integration

## Integration with Locals

### Turkish Hospitality

Turks are famously hospitable:
- Immediate welcome to new community members
- Tea/coffee offered freely
- Help given generously
- Social hierarchy friendly
- Direct, honest communication

### Learning Turkish

**Why Learn:**
- Opens doors with locals
- Appreciation shown
- Practical (not everyone speaks English)
- Can be fun
- Shows respect

**Where to Learn:**
- Language classes ($50-150/month)
-Language exchange groups
- YouTube channels
- Apps (Duolingo, etc.)
- Immersion (practice with locals)

**Reality:** Most expats learn 100-200 basic words and get by. Fluency not necessary but helpful.

### Cultural Respect

**Do:**
✓ Show respect (please/thank you in Turkish)
✓ Dress modestly in non-touristy areas
✓ Remove shoes when invited home
✓ Ask before taking photos (of people)
✓ Appreciate Turkish culture/history
✓ Greet regularly ("Merhaba" = hello)

**Don't:**
✗ Disrespect religion/traditions
✗ Use left hand to eat (traditionally improper)
✗ Point directly at people
✗ Rush conversations
✗ Ignore greetings

**Most people:** Very forgiving to foreigners learning

## Utilities & Home Setup

Quick guide (detailed in [Utilities](/living-in-bodrum/utilities)):

- **Electricity:** Account setup 1-3 days
- **Water:** Usually included or simple setup
- **Internet:** Critical—test speeds, takes 1-2 days
- **Gas:** May not be available; some use portable tanks
- **Phone:** Get SIM card first week

## Getting Around

Quick guide (detailed in [Transportation](/living-in-bodrum/transportation)):

- **Car:** Optional/recommended
- **Dolmuş:** Cheap local transport ($1-2)
- **Taxi:** Reasonable (~$3-5 within Bodrum)
- **Bike/Scooter:** Popular option
- **Walking:** Possible in town center

## Healthcare Access

See [Healthcare System](/getting-started/healthcare), but quick reminder:

- Private clinics excellent
- Pharmacies on every corner
- Doctors English-speaking (usually)
- Costs very affordable
- Insurance required for residency

## Work Setup

If working remotely:

- **Internet speed:** Critical (get fiber if possible)
- **Coworking spaces:** Available if need professional setting
- **Visas:** Residence permit OK for remote work (grey area)
- **Time zones:** Europe-friendly, US overlap difficult
- **Taxes:** See [Work & Business](/work-and-business/taxes)

## Social Media & Communication

### Staying Connected

**With Home:**
- WhatsApp: Free messaging (recommend)
- Zoom: Reliable for calls
- Facebook: Connect with friends
- Instagram: Share your new life

**With Community:**
- Facebook Groups: Essential resource
- WhatsApp Groups: Expat networks usually have
- Meetup.com: Some organized events
- Instagram Local: Find activities, restaurants

### Internet Quality

- Generally good (50-100 Mbps available)
- Fiber available in parts
- Test before committing to rental
- Consider technology before signing lease
- Backup mobile internet helpful

## Mail & Packages

### Receiving Mail
- Turkish postal service works but slow
- Post office (Posta) throughout Bodrum
- Can receive packages (small fee)
- Important: Use proper address

### International Shipping
- DHL/FedEx/UPS available
- More expensive but reliable
- Address clearly in Turkish characters
- Use if important documents

### Mail Forwarding
- Can set up from home country
- Usually forwarded to emailed address
- Or physical forwarding service available

## Legal & Documentation

### Important Documents

**Keep safe (copies at home too):**
- Passport
- Residence permit
- Insurance documents
- Rental agreement
- Bank documents
- Medical records

**Cloud backup:** Digital copies on Google Drive/similar

### Registration

**Required to register:**
- At municipality (with address)
- With embassy (of your country)
- Insurance up to date
- Tax ID if working

### Ongoing Requirements

**Annually (typically):**
- Renew residence permit (or apply for 2-year)
- Update insurance
- File taxes (if applicable)
- Check visa/passport expiry

## Expat Adjustment Timeline

### Week 1
- Everything exciting/novel
- Settling into accommodation
- Honeymoon phase
- Sleep adjusting

### Weeks 2-4
- Reality sets in
- Bureaucracy frustrating
- Miss some home comforts
- Building routine

### Months 2-3
- Integration beginning
- Making friends
- Understanding rhythm
- Settling feeling

### Months 3-6
- Real community forming
- Challenges manageable
- Feeling at home
- Commitment clarifying

### 6+ Months
- Most expats fully settled
- Community established
- Bodrum feels like home
- Few regrets if wanted to stay

<Callout type="success">
**Reality:** 80% of expats who stay 6+ months never leave. The adjustment happens, the payoff comes, and they build deep roots in Bodrum.
</Callout>
